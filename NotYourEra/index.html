Nirvana

1987 - April 5, 1994

Nirvana is an iconic band from the grunge era known for hits like "Smells Like Teen Spirit" and "Come as You Are". With Kurt Cobain's signature vocals and unique musical style, they became one of the most influential bands in rock history. Although their journey ended tragically, Nirvana's musical legacy continues to inspire millions of fans around the world.

The Beatles

August 18, 1960 - April 10, 1970

The Beatles are a legendary British band consisting of John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison, and Ringo Starr. With hits like "Hey Jude" and "Let It Be," they changed the face of world music. As one of the most influential bands in history, The Beatles remain a musical icon to this day.

David Bowie

January 8, 1947 - January 10, 2016

David Bowie, English music icon, known for personas like Ziggy Stardust, blended glam rock, pop, and experimental styles. Hits like "Space Oddity," "Heroes," and "Let's Dance" showcased his innovation, captivating fans globally. His legacy as a singer, songwriter, and actor remains a timeless inspiration for artists and musicians.

Queen

Since June 28, 1970

Formed in 1970, Queen is a British band led by Freddie Mercury. Blending rock, pop, opera, and progressive styles, they produced hits like "Bohemian Rhapsody" and "We Will Rock You." With mesmerizing stage shows and vocal harmonies, Queen became legendary. Their influence and commercial triumph cemented their status as a revered rock band, inspiring fans worldwide.
